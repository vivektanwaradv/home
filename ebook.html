About the Ebook

This ebook serves as a practical guide for individuals dealing with a cheque bounce (dishonour of a cheque), specifically focusing on the legal procedures required under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881. It is designed to simplify the law and provide a step-by-step process for drafting and sending a formal legal notice.

1. Understanding Cheque Bounce

A cheque bounce occurs when a bank returns a cheque unpaid. Common reasons include:

  • Funds Insufficient: Lack of enough money in the account.
  • Account Blocked/Closed: The account is inactive or restricted.
  • Signature Mismatch: The signature does not match bank records.

2. Legal Requirements and Timelines

Before filing a court case, the law mandates giving the defaulter a final chance to pay. Two critical rules apply:

  • The 30-Day Rule: The legal notice must be sent within 30 days of receiving the "Return Memo" from the bank.
  • The 15-Day Warning: The notice must give the defaulter 15 days from the date of receipt to make the payment. Legal action can only proceed if they fail to pay within this window.

3. Anatomy of a Legal Notice

The guide breaks down the structure of a notice into 10 essential parts, including:

  • Sender Details: Advocate’s letterhead and the exact date.
  • Subject Line: A clear heading citing Section 138 of the N.I. Act.
  • The Background: Explaining the reason for the debt (e.g., a friendly loan or business transaction) and specific cheque details.
  • The Demand: A formal demand for the exact amount and a warning of criminal prosecution if unpaid.

4. Practical Resources

  • Sample Format: The ebook provides a template using a hypothetical case involving a Rs. 3,00,000/- loan to help users draft their own notice.
  • Delivery Advice: It emphasizes sending the notice via Registered Post (AD) or Speed Post to maintain valid proof of delivery for court purposes.
